Peer Review Process – Journal of Computing and Numerical Sciences

Overview
The Journal of Computing and Numerical Sciences (JCNS) follows a rigorous double-blind peer review process to ensure the publication of high-quality, original, and scientifically sound research. Both authors and reviewers remain anonymous throughout the review process to maintain impartiality and fairness.
Initial Screening
Upon submission, all manuscripts undergo an initial evaluation by the Editorial Office.
The manuscript is checked for:

Scope and relevance to the journal
Basic quality and structure
Compliance with author guidelines
Plagiarism screening

Manuscripts that do not meet the journal’s standards or fall outside its scope may be rejected at this stage.
Reviewer Assignment
Suitable manuscripts are assigned to at least two independent expert reviewers in the relevant field.
Reviewers are selected based on their expertise, research background, and publication record.
The review process follows a double-blind system, ensuring anonymity of both authors and reviewers.

Review Evaluation Criteria

Reviewers evaluate manuscripts based on
Originality and novelty of the research
Technical and scientific quality
Methodological soundness
Clarity of presentation and organization
Relevance to the field of computing and numerical sciences
Significance of results and contribution to knowledge
Review Outcomes
Based on reviewers’ comments, the editorial decision may be
Accept without changes
Minor revisions required
Major revisions required
Reject

Authors receiving revision requests must address all reviewer comments and resubmit the revised manuscript within the specified timeframe.
Revision and Re-Review
Revised manuscripts may be returned to the original reviewers for further evaluation.
Authors must provide a detailed response to reviewers outlining changes made.
Failure to adequately address comments may lead to rejection.
Final Decision
The Editor-in-Chief or Handling Editor makes the final decision based on reviewers’ recommendations and the quality of revisions.
The decision is communicated to the authors along with reviewers’ feedback.
Confidentiality
All submitted manuscripts are treated as confidential documents.
Reviewers must not share or use any information from the manuscript for personal advantage.
Ethical Considerations
JCNS follows strict policies regarding plagiarism, data fabrication, and duplicate publication.
Any ethical concerns identified during the review process are handled according to international publishing standards.
Review Timeline
Initial screening: 1–2 weeks
Peer review: 2–4 weeks
Final decision: 4–8 weeks (approx.)
Transparency and Integrity
The journal ensures a fair, unbiased, and transparent peer review process.
All decisions are made based on academic merit and ethical standards.
Conflicts of interest are strictly managed.
