Publication Ethics and Malpractice Statement – Journal of Computing and Numerical Sciences

Overview
The Journal of Computing and Numerical Sciences (JCNS) is committed to maintaining the highest standards of publication ethics and actively prevents all forms of publication malpractice.

All stakeholders involved in the publication process—including authors, editors, reviewers, and the publisher—are expected to adhere to established ethical guidelines and best practices in scholarly publishing.

Duties of Authors

Originality and Plagiarism
Authors must ensure that their submitted work is entirely original.
Any use of previously published material must be properly cited or quoted.
Plagiarism in any form is strictly prohibited and may lead to rejection or retraction of the manuscript.
Multiple, Redundant, or Concurrent Publication
Authors must not submit the same manuscript to more than one journal simultaneously.
Submitting previously published work without proper acknowledgment is considered unethical.
Authorship of the Paper
Only individuals who have made significant contributions to the research should be listed as authors.
All co-authors must review and approve the final version of the manuscript before submission.
Data Accuracy and Integrity
Authors are responsible for presenting accurate and reliable data.
Fabrication, falsification, or manipulation of data is strictly unacceptable.
Disclosure and Conflicts of Interest
Authors must disclose any financial or personal conflicts of interest that could influence the research outcomes.

Duties of Editors

Fair Decision-Making
Editors evaluate manuscripts solely based on academic merit.
Decisions are made without discrimination based on race, gender, nationality, or institutional affiliation.
Confidentiality
Editors must keep all information related to submitted manuscripts confidential.
Information may only be shared with the corresponding author, reviewers, and editorial team.
Conflict of Interest
Editors must recuse themselves from handling manuscripts where a conflict of interest exists.
Publication Decisions
Editors are responsible for final publication decisions based on reviewers’ recommendations and journal policies.

Duties of Reviewers

Confidentiality
Manuscripts received for review must be treated as confidential documents.
Objectivity
Reviews should be conducted objectively and professionally.
Constructive feedback must be provided to help improve the manuscript.
Acknowledgment of Sources
Reviewers should identify relevant published work not cited by the authors.
Conflict of Intrest
Reviewers must decline review assignments if any conflict of interest exists.

Ethical Oversight

Plagiarism Detection
All submissions are secreened using plagiarism detection tools.
Manuscripts exceeding acceptable similarity thresholds will be rejected.
Research Misconduct
Allegations of misconduct (e.g., plagiarism, data fabrication) will be throughly investigated.
Appropriate actions, including rejection or retraction, will be taken if misconduct is confirmed.
Corrections and Retractions
Errors identified after publication will be corrected through errata or corrigends.
In case of serious ethical voilations, articles may be formally retracted.
Copyright and Licensing
Authors retain copyright of their work.
Authors grant the journal the right to publish and distribute the article.
Licensing terms (e.g., open access policies) will be clearly stated on the journal website.
Compliance with Ethical Standards
JCNS adheres to intentionally recognized ethical guidelines to ensure integrity, transparency, and trust in schloarly publishing.
