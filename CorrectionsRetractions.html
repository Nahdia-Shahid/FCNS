Corrections & Retractions – Journal of Computing and Numerical Sciences

Policy Statement
The Journal of Computing and Numerical Sciences (JCNS) is committed to preserving the accuracy, integrity, and reliability of the scholarly record. When errors, ethical concerns, or research misconduct are identified in published articles, the journal will take appropriate corrective action in accordance with internationally recognized publication ethics and editorial best practices.
Corrections
Minor errors that do not affect the scientific validity, interpretation, or conclusions of a published article may be corrected through an official correction notice. Such corrections may include typographical errors, author affiliations, contact information, acknowledgments, or formatting issues. The correction will remain permanently linked to the original published article.
Retractions
A published article may be retracted if there is clear evidence of research misconduct, plagiarism, duplicate publication, fabricated or falsified data, unethical research practices, copyright infringement, or significant errors that invalidate the study's findings. Retraction is intended to maintain the integrity of the scholarly literature and to inform readers of unreliable research.
Grounds for Retraction
Retraction may be considered under circumstances including, but not limited to:
  • Research misconduct or scientific fraud.
  • Plagiarism or duplicate publication.
  • Fabrication or falsification of research data.
  • Serious methodological or analytical errors.
  • Unethical research involving humans or animals.
  • Undisclosed conflicts of interest affecting the validity of the work.
  • Copyright violations or legal concerns.
Retraction Procedure
When concerns are raised regarding a published article, the Editorial Office will conduct a fair and confidential investigation. Authors will be given an opportunity to respond before any final decision is made. Where necessary, independent experts, reviewers, or members of the Editorial Board may be consulted during the investigation process.
Retraction Notice
If an article is retracted, JCNS will publish a formal retraction notice explaining the reason for the retraction. The original article will remain available in the journal archive for transparency but will be clearly marked as "Retracted" to inform readers of its publication status.
Expressions of Concern
In situations where an investigation is ongoing and conclusive evidence is not yet available, the journal may publish an Expression of Concern to alert readers while the matter is being investigated.
Author Responsibilities
Authors are expected to promptly notify the Editorial Office if they discover significant errors or inaccuracies in their published work. Authors should cooperate fully with the journal during any investigation and assist in issuing corrections or retractions where appropriate.
Editorial Responsibilities
The Editorial Board will evaluate all reported concerns objectively, confidentially, and without bias. Decisions regarding corrections, expressions of concern, or retractions will be based solely on available evidence, publication ethics, and internationally accepted editorial guidelines.
Journal Commitment
JCNS is committed to safeguarding the integrity of the scholarly record by ensuring that all published content remains accurate, trustworthy, and ethically sound. The journal continuously follows best editorial practices to maintain confidence in the research it publishes.
