Conflict of Interest – Journal of Computing and Numerical Sciences

Policy Statement
The Journal of Computing and Numerical Sciences (JCNS) is committed to maintaining transparency, integrity, and fairness throughout the publication process. Authors, reviewers, editors, and editorial board members are required to disclose any actual, potential, or perceived conflicts of interest that could influence their professional judgment or decision-making.
Authors
Authors must disclose all financial, institutional, personal, or professional relationships that could be perceived as influencing the research findings. Sources of funding, sponsorship, grants, or any competing interests should be clearly stated within the manuscript at the time of submission.
Reviewers
Reviewers must decline invitations to review manuscripts whenever a conflict of interest exists. This includes collaborations with the authors, institutional affiliations, financial interests, or any personal relationships that could compromise an objective and unbiased review.
Editors
Editors shall manage submitted manuscripts impartially and must recuse themselves from editorial decisions whenever a conflict of interest exists. In such cases, another qualified editor will be assigned to oversee the review and publication process.
Editorial Board Members
Editorial Board members are expected to perform their responsibilities with independence, confidentiality, and impartiality. Any competing interests that may affect editorial judgment must be disclosed immediately to the Editor-in-Chief.
Types of Conflicts of Interest
Conflicts of interest may include, but are not limited to:
  • Financial interests or funding sources.
  • Employment or institutional affiliations.
  • Personal, family, or professional relationships.
  • Academic competition or collaboration.
  • Ownership of patents, intellectual property, or commercial interests.
Disclosure Requirements
All relevant conflicts of interest must be disclosed before manuscript review and publication. If no competing interests exist, authors should include the statement: "The authors declare that there are no conflicts of interest regarding this publication."
Management of Conflicts
When a conflict of interest is identified, the Editorial Office will evaluate its significance and take appropriate action. This may include assigning independent reviewers or editors, requesting additional disclosures, or declining the manuscript if impartiality cannot be ensured.
Failure to Disclose
Failure to disclose relevant conflicts of interest may result in manuscript rejection, withdrawal of a published article, publication of a correction, or other editorial actions deemed appropriate according to the journal's publication ethics policies.
Journal Commitment
JCNS is dedicated to ensuring transparency and maintaining the highest standards of ethical publishing. All participants involved in the publication process are expected to uphold honesty, integrity, and professional responsibility in accordance with internationally accepted publishing practices.
